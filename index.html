Understanding Etsy Fees & Maximizing Seller Profitability

Selling handmade goods, vintage items, and craft supplies on Etsy provides artisans with an international marketplace. However, understanding the exact financial mechanics behind every sale is essential to building a profitable e-commerce business.

Our free Etsy Fee & Profit Calculator simplifies this process by providing an itemized breakdown of your transaction expenses, allowing you to set competitive prices while safeguarding your profit margins.

A Complete Breakdown of Etsy Seller Fees in 2026

To accurately price your merchandise, you must understand the core fee structures enforced by Etsy for US-based stores:

  • Listing Fee ($0.20 USD): Etsy charges a flat rate of twenty cents to list an item for sale.
  • Transaction Fee (6.5%): Etsy takes a 6.5% commission on the entire transaction amount including shipping.
  • Payment Processing Fee (3% + $0.25 USD): Standard US processing rate for transactions.
  • Offsite Ads Fee (12% to 15%): Mandatory or optional based on shop sales volume.

How to Calculate Your True Etsy Profit Margin

Determining your net profitability requires looking beyond marketplace fees. Your net income formula follows this mathematical breakdown:

Net Profit = Total Revenue - (Etsy Deductions + Cost of Goods Sold + Actual Shipping Costs)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Does Etsy charge fees on shipping cost?
Yes, Etsy's 6.5% transaction fee applies to the total amount collected including shipping.
What is the Etsy Offsite Ads fee and who pays it?
Offsite Ads fees are charged when a buyer clicks an external ad and makes a purchase. Standard sellers pay 15%, while high-volume sellers with over $10k in sales pay 12%.
How does the Etsy payment processing fee work?
Etsy charges a processing fee of 3% + $0.25 per transaction in the US. This covers credit card and digital wallet payments.
